## Topic/Problem
Comprehensive troubleshooting guide for common issues in classic cars, specifically applicable to Triumph vehicles. Originally written for MGs but provides a basic plan of attack for problems with almost any classic car. Covers systematic diagnosis for brakes, engine, steering, fuel, electrical, clutch, and cooling systems.

## Solution/Information

### Brakes Problem Solvers

| **PROBLEM** | **CAUSE** | **CURE** |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Excessive brake pedal travel | 1. Brakes need adjusting or replacement.<br/>2. Air in system.<br/>3. Faulty master cylinder.<br/>4. Leaking or contaminated fluid. | 1. Adjust or renew brake shoes.<br/>2. Bleed hydraulic system.<br/>3. Fit new master cylinder.<br/>4. Bleed hydraulic system. |
| Brake fade | 1. Incorrect pad or lining material.<br/>2. Old or contaminated fluid.<br/>3. Excessive use of brakes or car overloaded. | 1. Fit new pads or shoes.<br/>2. Renew brake fluid.<br/>3. Check vehicle load. |
| Spongy brake pedal | 1. Air in hydraulic system.<br/>2. Shoes badly lined or distorted.<br/>3. Faulty hydraulic cylinder. | 1. Bleed system.<br/>2. Fit new pads or shoes.<br/>3. Check hydraulic circuit. |
| Brake pedal too hard | 1. Seized wheel cylinder or caliper piston<br/>2. Glazed friction material | 1. Replace seized component.<br/>2. Fit new shoes/pads. |
| Brake pedal requires pumping or loss of pedal | 1. Brakes wrongly adjusted<br/>2. Air in hydraulic system<br/>3. Fluid leak from component or brake pipe<br/>4. Loss of fluid from master cylinder | 1. Adjust brakes.<br/>2. Bleed system.<br/>3. Check hydraulic circuit and replace parts as necessary.<br/>4. Check hydraulic circuit and replace parts as necessary. |
| Brakes grab when applied | 1. Contaminated friction material<br/>2. Wrong linings fitted<br/>3. Scored drums or discs | 1. Replace (don't clean) pads or shoes.<br/>2. Replace (don't clean) pads or shoes.<br/>3. Fit new drum or disc. |
| Brake squeal | 1. Worn retaining pins (disc)<br/>2. Faulty damping shims or shoe retaining clips<br/>3. Dust in drum<br/>4. Loose backplate or caliper | 1. Fit new pins.<br/>2. Fit new shims or clips.<br/>3. Remove dust from drums and shoe.<br/>4. Tighten caliper or backplate. |
| Brake shudder | 1. No clearance at master cylinder operating rod<br/>2. Shoe tension springs either broken or weak<br/>3. Wheel cylinder or caliper piston seizing<br/>4. Faulty self-adjusting mechanism<br/>5. Seized handbrake mechanism | 1. Adjust rod if possible.<br/>2. Replace tension springs<br/>3. Fit new caliper or cylinder.<br/>4. Check mechanism.<br/>5. Check handbrake operation. |
| Brake pull to one side only | 1. Contaminated friction material on one side (grease, oil or brake fluid<br/>2. Loose backplate<br/>3. Seized cylinder<br/>4. Faulty suspension or steering | 1. Replace shoes/pads all around.<br/>2. Tighten backplate.<br/>3. Replace seized cylinder.<br/>4. Check suspension and steering. |
| Handbrake ineffective | 1. Worn rear shoes or pads<br/>2. Brakes require adjusting<br/>3. Faulty handbrake linkage<br/>4. Cable or rod requires adjustment | 1. Fit new pads/shoes.<br/>2. Adjust brakes<br/>3. Check linkage and operating mechanism.<br/>4. Adjust cable or rod. |
| Servo (where fitted) late in operation | 1. blocked filter<br/>2. Bad vacuum sealing or restricted air inlet | 1. Clean or replace filter.<br/>2. Tighten vacuum hose connections and check hoses |
| Loss of servo action when braking heavily | 1. Air leak in servo-vacuum low | 1. Either overhaul servo or replace |
| Loss of fluid (servo only) | 1. Seal failure<br/>2. Scored servo bores<br/>3. Damaged or corroded fluid pipes | 1. Replace or overhaul servo.<br/>2. Replace or overhaul servo.<br/>3. Inspect and fit new pipes. |

### Engine Problem Solvers

| **PROBLEM** | **CAUSE** | **CURE** |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Lack of compression | 1. Faulty valve seat, excessive wear in stem or guide.<br/>2. Faulty head gasket.<br/>3. Worn pistons, rings and bores. | 1. Recut seat and valve, fit new guide and valve.<br/>2. Fit new gasket or reface head.<br/>3. Either fit new rings, or if badly worn, fit new pistons and rings, and have block rebored. |
| Piston slap | 1. As #3 above. | 1. As #3 above. |
| Smoke from exhaust<br/>Lack of power | 1. As above.<br/>2. Blocked crankcase breather. | 1. As above.<br/>2. Check breathing apparatus. |
| Noisy tappet (with correct clearance) | 1. Wear in rocker pad face and/or rocker sleeve and shaft (OHV).<br/>2. Worn cam follower (OHC). | 1. Reface pad surface, replace rockers or shaft (OHV).<br/>2. Fit new followers (OHC). |
| Big-end knock | 1. Wear between big-end shell and crankcase. Wrong torque on bolts. | 1. Depending on wear, fit new shells, regrind crankshaft and check torque |
| Mains rumble | 1. Wear between main bearing shells and crankshaft. | 1. As Above |
| Cam follower tap | 1. Camshaft worn or follower dished. | 1. Examine and replace followers or camshaft. Or Both |
| Knocking when clutch depressed. Movement at crank pulley | 1. Excessive crankshaft end-float. Wear between crank and thrust washer. | 1. Fit new thrust washers and recheck clearance. |
| Clattering from front of engine | 1. Excessive or slack timing chain, worn chain tensioner. | 1. Fit new chain and tensioner. Adjust chain where necessary. |
| Small-end or gudgeon pin knock | 1. Excessive wear between gudgeon pin and con-rod. | 1. Fit new bush or con-rod. |
| Lack of oil pressure | 1. Excessive wear in crankshaft journals.<br/>2. Faulty oil pump.<br/>3. Blocked oil pick-up strainer.<br/>4. Faulty pressure-relief valve.<br/>5. Blocked oil filter.<br/>6. Lack of oil | 1. Overhaul engine.<br/>2. Fit new pump.<br/>3. Clean pick-up.<br/>4. Fit new relief valve.<br/>5. Fit new filter.<br/>6. Install fresh oil. |
| Oil leaks | 1. Sump gaskets or packings.<br/>2. Front and rear crankshaft oil seal.<br/>3. rocker or camshaft gasket.<br/>4. Oil filter. | 1. Fit new gaskets.<br/>2. Fit new seals.<br/>3. Fit new gasket.<br/>4. Check filter seal. |
| Lack of power (engine in good condition) | 1. Faulty ignition timing. Faulty sparking plugs, points or condenser. Wrong valve clearance. | 1. Tune engine. |

### Electrical Problem Solvers

| **PROBLEM** | **CAUSE** | **CURE** |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| **STARTER** |
| Starter doesn't turn<br/>(lights dim) | 1. Battery flat or worn<br/>2. Bad connection in battery circuit | 1. Charge or fit new battery.<br/>2. Check all feed and earth connections. |
| starter doesn't turn<br/>(lights stay bright) | 1. Faulty ignition switch<br/>2. Broken starter circuit | 1. Check switch.<br/>2. Check starter circuit. |
| Solenoid switch chatters | 1. Flat battery | 1. Charge or replace battery |
| Starter just spins | 1. Bendix gear sticking | 1. Remove starter and clean or replace Bendix gear. |
| **CHARGING CIRCUIT** |
| Low or no charge rate | 1. Broken or slipping drive belt<br/>2. Poor connections on or faulty alternator. | 1. Fit new belt.<br/>2. Check and replace alternator. |
| **LIGHTING CIRCUIT** |
| No lights (or very dim) | 1. Flat or faulty battery, bad battery connections | 1. Check battery and connections. |
| side and rear lights inoperative although stoplights and flashers work | 1. Fuse blown | 1. Fit correct value fuse |
| One lamp fails | 1. Blown bulb<br/>2. Poor bulb contact<br/>3. Bad earth connection<br/>4. Broken feed | 1. Fit new bulb.<br/>2. Check connections.<br/>3. Check connection.<br/>4. Check feed. |
| Flasher warning bulb stays on or flashers twice as fast | 1. Faulty bulb or connection on front or rear of offending side | 1. Fit new bulb, make good connection. |
| Lights dim when idling or at low speed | 1. Loose drive belt<br/>2. Flat battery<br/>3. Faulty charging circuit | 1. Tighten belt.<br/>2. Check charge output and battery.<br/>3. Check charge output and battery. |
| One dim light | 1. Blackened bulb<br/>2. Bad earth<br/>3. Tarnished reflector | 1. fit new bulb or sealed-beam.<br/>2. Check earth connections.<br/>3. Fit new bulb or sealed-beam. |
| **WINDSCREEN WIPERS** |
| Wipers do not work | 1. Blown fuse<br/>2. Poor connection<br/>3. Faulty switch<br/>4. Faulty motor | 1. Fit fuse.<br/>2. Check connections<br/>3. Check switch.<br/>4. remove and examine motor. |
| Motor operates slowly | 1. Excessive resistance in circuit or wiper drive<br/>2. worn brushes | 1. Check wiper circuit.<br/>2. Remove motor and check brushes. |
